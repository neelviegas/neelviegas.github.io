02

Thrust Analysis Tool

McGill Rocket Team
Role: Injector team lead Stack: Python · Matplotlib · NumPy Timeline: May 2026 – present

A python pipeline that takes in hotfire test data and outputs the coefficient of combustion efficiency. Calculations involved: mass flow rate, regression law, and chamber pressure & temp.

It ingests raw test-stand telemetry from a hybrid hotfire and turns it into a clean set of analysis graphs, handling the messy realities of test data — like substituting a faulted thermocouple (tc4 in for tc5) — so the output is trustworthy enough to feed downstream efficiency calculations.

Result — HF1.1
Total oxidizer 11.70 kg · peak 1.86 kg/s · average 1.69 kg/s.
03

Automatic Curtains

Personal Project
Role: Solo — design, CAD, circuit, code Stack: SolidWorks · Arduino UNO · 3D print Timeline: Jun 2026 – present

I live next to a building whose lights, unfortunately for me, never turn off. That said, I like waking up to the sun without compromising my sleep. This led me to build automatic curtains.

Custom gear train and servo mount designed in SolidWorks. Powered and controlled by an Arduino UNO that opens the curtains in the morning and closes them at night.

  • Designed the drive gears and servo/electronics mounts in SolidWorks, then 3D printed them.
  • Built the circuit around an Arduino UNO with a timed open/close routine.
  • Iterated on the gear set until the torque and fit were right.
04

Vortex Injector Redesign

McGill Rocket Team
Role: Injector project (member → co-lead) Stack: Siemens NX · GD&T Timeline: Aug 2025 – May 2026

In response to a growing body of research advocating for vortex injector hole angles closer to 45 degrees, I was tasked with updating our bench-scale and full-scale vortex injectors to optimize oxidizer–fuel mixing in our hybrid rocket propulsion system.

I presented my research to the team, then redesigned both injectors in NX and produced new manufacturing drawings — including the full GD&T, sections, and detail views needed to actually make the parts.

  • Researched and justified the ~45° hole-angle change against prior geometry.
  • Rebuilt the bench-scale and full-scale injector CAD in Siemens NX.
  • Authored new drawings with tolerances, seal callouts, and detail scales.
